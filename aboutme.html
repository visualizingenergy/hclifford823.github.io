About Me


During my time as a graduate research assitant, I have found enjoyment in learning how to code and applying data analysis to different aspects of climate science. I devoted much of my time to learning new techniques and implementations for python code in the world of paleoclimate time series, specifically related to ice cores. When I am not coding, writing scientific literature, or in the lab, my research has brought me to some beautiful places including Mt. Everest (Nepal), Central Andes (Peru), and the Greenland ice sheet.

When I am not working, I like to drink coffee, hike, ski, swim in the ocean, and cuddle with my cats.
